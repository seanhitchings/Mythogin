Quantum: Local vs Global

Quantum systems behave locally when measured — yet their full description may only exist globally across space.

Audio Listen Download
Transcript Read Download

Discussion

Quantum mechanics challenges the idea that physical reality is fully local.

Classical physics assumes that objects have properties determined at each point in space. Quantum theory instead describes systems with wavefunctions that can span entire regions.

Entangled particles exhibit correlations that cannot be explained by purely local variables. The global state contains information that no single part fully holds on its own.

Entanglement

▾

Two particles can share a single quantum state. Measuring one instantly constrains the other, even across large distances.

Local measurements

▾

Each experiment yields a definite local outcome — but the statistical structure emerges from the global wavefunction.

Bell’s theorem

▾

Experimental violations of Bell inequalities rule out simple local hidden-variable explanations.

  • Claim: the global state exceeds local description.
  • Tension: measurement is local; reality may not be.
  • Implication: separability is limited.

Impact

The local–global divide reshapes physics, computation, and philosophy.

Quantum information

▾

Quantum computing and cryptography exploit global entangled states to achieve capabilities impossible for classical local systems.

Foundations of physics

▾

Interpretations of quantum mechanics hinge on how one reconciles global wavefunctions with local experience.

Holism vs reductionism

▾

The debate challenges purely reductionist views, suggesting that wholes can contain irreducible structure.

Fix

Three ways to think clearly about quantum locality.

1) Distinguish description from outcome

▾

The wavefunction describes possibilities globally; outcomes appear locally.

2) Focus on correlations

▾

Quantum theory predicts correlations more fundamentally than individual properties.

3) Accept limited separability

▾

Some systems cannot be fully understood by analyzing parts independently.
